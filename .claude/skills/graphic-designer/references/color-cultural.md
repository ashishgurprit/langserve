# Cultural Color Psychology Reference

## Critical Principle

**Color meanings are NOT universal.** Western color psychology does not apply globally. Always research target audience's cultural context.

## Color by Region

### Red

| Region | Associations | Usage | Avoid |
|--------|-------------|--------|-------|
| **Western** (US, EU) | Passion, danger, urgency, love, excitement, anger | Sale signs, CTAs, Valentine's Day, stop signs | Excessive use (aggression), formal docs |
| **Eastern** (China, Japan, Korea) | Prosperity, luck, celebration, joy, happiness | Weddings (China), envelopes (money gifts), festivals | Funerals (some regions), names written in red (death) |
| **Latin America** | Celebration, religion, passion, revolution | Christmas, political movements, festivals | Depends on country (research specific) |
| **Middle East** | Sacrifice, danger, caution | Warning signs, traditional textiles | Religious contexts (check local customs) |
| **India** | Purity, marriage, fertility, strength | Bridal wear, festivals (Holi), religious ceremonies | Funerals (use white or black) |
| **South Africa** | Mourning (in some tribes), also political movements | Struggle history (apartheid), traditional contexts | Depends on ethnic group |

**Brand Examples:**
- Coca-Cola (universal energy, excitement)
- Red Bull (energy, extreme sports)
- Netflix (entertainment, excitement)
- YouTube (urgency, attention)

### Blue

| Region | Associations | Usage | Avoid |
|--------|-------------|--------|-------|
| **Western** | Trust, security, calm, professionalism, corporate, stability | Finance, healthcare, tech, corporate identity | Overuse (cold, impersonal) |
| **Eastern** | Immortality, spirituality (Buddhism), coolness, calm | Religious contexts, traditional art | Some associations with mourning (Korea) |
| **Latin America** | Serenity, trust, mourning (light blue in Argentina) | Professional contexts, sky/water themes | Check country-specific meanings |
| **Middle East** | Protection, spirituality, heaven, safety | Religious contexts (Mosques), evil eye protection | Overly casual use in formal settings |
| **India** | Divinity (Krishna), courage, masculinity | Religious depictions, patriotic contexts | Overly casual contexts |
| **Global Corporate** | Most universally safe color for brands | Finance, healthcare, SaaS, B2B | Becoming generic due to overuse |

**Brand Examples:**
- Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter (trust, social)
- IBM, Intel, HP (technology, reliability)
- Ford, Volkswagen (automotive trust)
- Chase, Visa, PayPal (financial security)

## Special Considerations

### Neon/Fluorescent Colors

**Global:** Modern, youthful, energetic, attention-grabbing, tech, Gen-Z aesthetic
**Examples:** Y2K revival, cyberpunk aesthetic, sports brands
**Brands:** Off-White, Mountain Dew, Monster Energy

### Pastels

**Global:** Soft, gentle, calming, feminine (traditional), modern minimalism
**Examples:** Scandinavian design, wellness brands, baby products
**Brands:** Glossier, Casper, Sweaty Betty

### Metallic (Gold, Silver, Bronze)

- **Gold:** Luxury, wealth, success (universal, but divine in India/Middle East)
- **Silver:** Technology, modernity, sophistication (global)
- **Bronze:** Achievement, heritage, craftsmanship (global)

## Color Combination Strategies by Culture

### Western Color Palettes

**Corporate/Trust:** Blue + Gray
**Luxury:** Black + Gold
**Eco/Health:** Green + White + Brown
**Tech:** Blue + White + Gray
**Youth/Energy:** Bright multi-color (Google, eBay)

### Eastern Color Palettes

**Traditional Chinese:** Red + Gold (prosperity)
**Japanese Minimalism:** White + Black + Natural wood tones
**Korean Modern:** Soft pastels + white (K-beauty aesthetic)

### Middle Eastern Palettes

**Traditional:** Gold + Deep Blue + Red (mosques, textiles)
**Modern Gulf:** White + Gold + Marble tones (luxury)

### Indian Palettes

**Festive:** Bright multi-colors (Holi influence)
**Religious:** Saffron + Red + Yellow
**Modern Indian Design:** Jewel tones (emerald, sapphire, ruby)

### Latin American Palettes

**Traditional:** Earth tones + bright accents (indigenous influence)
**Festive:** Vibrant multi-colors (Carnival, Day of the Dead)
**Modern:** Bold colors + white (tropical modernism)

## Accessibility & Cultural Color

**Critical:** Color blindness affects 1 in 12 men (8%), 1 in 200 women (0.5%)

**Types:**
- **Deuteranopia** (most common): Red-green confusion
- **Protanopia:** Red-green (different from above)
- **Tritanopia:** Blue-yellow confusion

**Solutions:**
- Never rely on color alone (use icons, text labels, patterns)
- Test with simulators (Stark plugin for Figma, Color Oracle)
- Use high contrast (WCAG AA: 4.5:1, AAA: 7:1)
- Avoid red/green as only differentiators (traffic lights also use position + shape)

## Research Resources

**Before Finalizing Color Palette:**
1. **Google:** "[Country] color symbolism"
2. **Academic:** "Color Psychology in [Culture]" (JSTOR, Google Scholar)
3. **Local Consultants:** Hire native designers for review
4. **Test:** User testing with target demographic (not assumptions)

**Tools:**
- [Color Culture Report](https://www.colourlovers.com/) - Community insights
- [Global Color Survey](https://blog.xkcd.com/2010/05/03/color-survey-results/) - XKCD's massive survey
- Local market research (Pew Research, regional studies)

---

**Golden Rule:** When in doubt, test with the actual audience. Cultural color psychology is a guideline, not a law. Individual preferences, modern globalization, and context always matter more than generalizations.
